Mac and Cheese

Description

Mac and cheese is a classic comfort food consisting of macaroni pasta and a cheese sauce. It's beloved by many for its creamy, cheesy flavor and is a staple in American cuisine.

This dish can be made with a variety of cheeses and sometimes includes breadcrumbs or additional ingredients like bacon or vegetables. It can be baked or cooked on the stovetop, and variations exist worldwide.

Originating from Italy, the first modern recipe for mac and cheese was included in Elizabeth Raffald's 1769 book, "The Experienced English Housekeeper."

Ingredients

  • Macaroni pasta
  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Cheddar cheese
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Breadcrumbs (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the macaroni according to package instructions.
  2. Make a roux with butter and flour.
  3. Add milk to the roux and cook until thickened.
  4. Stir in cheese until melted and smooth.
  5. Combine the macaroni with the cheese sauce.
  6. Pour into a baking dish, top with breadcrumbs if using, and bake.
  7. Let cool slightly before serving.
